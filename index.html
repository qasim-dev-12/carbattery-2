How do you know your Car needs a battery change?

Your car may require a battery replacement if you notice signs like trouble starting, dim headlights, electrical problems (such as power windows not working), slow engine cranking, warning lights appearing on the dashboard, corrosion on the battery terminals, the battery being more than 3-5 years old, or a swollen battery case.

Trouble starting: If your car has trouble starting or takes longer than normal, it could be a sign that the battery is failing.

Dim headlights: Flickering or dim headlights may suggest the battery is weak and unable to provide enough power.

Electrical problems: Issues with electrical components like power windows, door locks, or the radio may point to battery trouble.

Slow engine cranking: If the engine cranks slowly during startup, it could indicate that the battery is near the end of its life.

Warning lights: The appearance of the battery or check engine light could signal battery-related problems.

Corrosion on battery terminals: Visible corrosion on the battery terminals often indicates a potential battery failure.

Age of the battery: If your battery is older than 3-5 years, it may be approaching the end of its lifespan and might need replacing.

Swollen or bloated battery case: A swollen or bloated battery case is a clear sign of internal damage and requires immediate replacement.
